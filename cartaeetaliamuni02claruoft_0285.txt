Llangenydd was the seat of an Alien Priory, founded probably in the reign of Stepheu, by Roget de
Bellomont, the Norman Conqueror of Gower, and by him attached to the Monastery of St. Taurinus. at
Evreux7 in Normandy‚ and dedicated to St. Kenydd, a Welsh Saint.

These Priories were of two kinds: one managing their own affairs and electing their own head; the
other a mere cell, the Prior of which was nominated by the Superior House. Llangenydd probably was
of the latter description, since one of the conditions on which it was restored to St. Taurinus by
Henry IV. was⁷ that suitable Priors should be chosen.
